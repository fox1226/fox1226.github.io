关于夏目漱石和他的心
About Natsume Soseki and his heart

Posted by 张 骏晓 (Fox) on July 11, 2017

「私の鼓動が停った時、あなたの胸に新しい命が宿る事ができるなら満足です。」ーーー『こころ』

　最近，一位旧友饶有兴致的跟我聊起了日本文学，于是一时兴起，连着读了两本夏目漱石的小说，其实最初听到夏目漱石这个名字是在童年看的柯南里，只记得是一个头像被印在钞票上大文豪。对这个名字产生兴趣是因为得知他在任课时期将“I love you”译成了「月が綺麗ですね」（今晚的月色真美）。这样富有诗意的译文淋漓尽致的展现了东方人的韵味，赞叹之余也暗暗记下以期有朝一日也能附庸风雅，然而当我向姑娘们说出这句话的时候得到的回答大多是：“哦，是么？”。

　　提到夏目漱石就不得不提到他的代表作「吾辈は猫である」（我是猫），这样一部从自家猫咪身上获得灵感的小说不仅使夏目漱石名声鹊起，也为当时过着拮据生活的他解决了燃眉之急。然而，令我着迷的却是他的另外一本小说——「こころ」(心)。

　　在高谈阔论这部小说之前，一定要先谈谈「私小説」。这是二十世纪以来日本文学发展出的体裁，取材于作者的自身经历，多以第一人称为视角采取自我暴露的叙述法，以详细的心理活动描写来表现人性。这种写实主义的风格在二十世纪成为主流，也迎合了战后日本悲怆的民族色彩。

　　「こころ」便是这样一部小说，小说以「我」为主人公，讲述了「我」与「先生」相遇相识的故事。小说前半段以「我」的视角来审视「先生」，「先生」的处事风格让我产生了浓厚的兴趣，同时也留下了许多谜团让我困惑不已。在前半段的铺垫之后，小说中后部「先生」的长信才是作者真正想要讲述的故事，在读信的过程中，不光是书中的「我」，读者也会自然而然的将前半段中「我」所描述的「先生」与信中「先生」所经历的故事结合起来，使「先生」这个人物渐渐丰满起来，通过「先生」信中的自述，以及大量细腻的心理描写直白的展现了人心的自私与极端。当「先生」发现成为原本自身所厌恶的人时，无法摆脱的痛苦与哀伤也随之而来。在经历了几十年的挣扎与自责之后，最终「先生」选择将希望寄托于「我」而结束了自己的生命。

　　「こころ」带给我们的不仅仅是一段悲伤的故事，夏目漱石以轻柔而精准的笔力描写了一个知识分子挣扎和无力的内心世界。文中对「先生」的心理剖析细致入微、精妙绝伦、撼人心魄。对于人心极端自私的部分，多数人选择回避或粉饰，书中却非常直白的写了出来。你我遇到同样的事件想必也会有与文中人相同的想法，这种强烈的代入感和对人性的直击想必不仅仅是夏目漱石，也是日本文学的魅力所在吧。
　　

「暗いものをじっと見つめて、その中からあなたの参考になるものをおつかみなさい。」（凝视那些阴暗的事物，抓住其中可供你参考的东西。） ーーー夏目漱石



Recently, an old friend discussed with me about Japanese novel; It gave me a whim to read Natsume. In fact, the first time I heard Natsume Soseki was in Detective Conan when I was a kid. The image of him then is just a great writer who is on the Japanese paper notes. He translated “I love you” to 「月が綺麗ですね」(What a beautiful moon tonight) when he was a teacher in school made me interesting in his article. Such a poetic translation most vividly shows the charm of Eastern people. So I kept this sentence in mind and looking forward to being arty on one day in the future. However, whenever I speak this sentence to a girl, the answer is always “Oh, Really?”

When we are taking about Natsume Soseki, we have to mention his masterpiece「吾辈は猫である」(I am a cat). The novel which inspired by his cat is not only made Natsume being famous but also bring him a considerable income. However, the book which makes me fascinated is the other one, 「こころ」(Kokoro).

Before I start to talk about this novel, I have to say something about 「私小説」(I novel). It is a literary genre begun at 20th where the events in the story correspond to events in the author’s life. The form reflected greater individuality and a less constrained method of writing. From its beginnings, the “I-Novel” was a genre that also was meant to expose the dark side of society or the dark side of the author’s life. The realistic style became popular in 20th and just catered the national sentiment of Japanese after the war.

The novel Kokoro is such an “I Novel”. The book shows the story about “me” and “sensei”. The first half of story is described “sensei” by “me”. The lifestyle made “me” have a firm interested in “sensei” meanwhile I found a bunch of mystery bother me a lot. After the foreshadowing from “me”, the letter in the second half is the true story given by the writer. During “me” reading the letter, the reader like you and me can also combine the personality mentioned in the first half and the story in the letter which made the character more real. Through the self-description in the letter of “sensei”’, the selfish and extreme is unfolded by an enormous amount of delicate psychological description. When “sensei”’ found that he just became a person who he scorned before, the painful and sadness can’t get rid of was coming with that. After decades of struggle and self-blame, “sensei” decided to put his hope in me and finished his life.

Kokoro is not only about a sad story, Natsume described an inner world with struggle and powerless by his gentle but accurate word. The nuanced, exquisite psychological analysis in this novel shocking the everyone read it. For the part of extremely selfish in our inner world, most of the people choose to avoid or whitewash. The book is very straightforward to write it out. If the story in the novel happened to you and me, we might choose to do the same thing like “sensei”. This kind of sense of substitution and the directness of human nature must not only the charm of Natsume Soseki, but also the Japanese literature.
